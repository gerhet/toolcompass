Master Your Mortgage with Precision

Buying a home is likely the largest financial transaction of your life. Understanding exactly how your monthly payments are split between principal and interest is crucial for long-term financial health. The Mortgage Splitter provides a real-time, visual breakdown of your loan amortization—helping you make informed decisions about refinancing, extra payments, or loan terms.

⚠️ Financial Disclaimer: This tool provides estimates for educational purposes only. Results are not financial advice. Consult a licensed mortgage professional before making lending decisions. ToolCompass is not a lender and does not guarantee loan approval or rates.

Pro tips: Use the sliders to see how extra payments or rate changes affect your timeline. A difference of just 0.5% in interest can save you over $30,000 on a 30-year loan. Export your schedule to compare scenarios side-by-side.

Why Visualize Principal vs. Interest?

Early mortgage payments are heavily weighted toward interest. Seeing this split visually helps you understand the true cost of borrowing and motivates strategies like bi-weekly payments or lump-sum principal reductions. Our calculator updates instantly as you adjust inputs—no page reloads, no waiting.

The monthly payment is calculated using the standard amortization formula: M = P[r(1+r)^n]/[(1+r)^n-1], where P is principal, r is monthly interest rate, and n is number of payments. This accounts for both principal reduction and interest accrual over your loan term.

In amortizing loans, each payment covers both interest and principal. Early payments are mostly interest because they're calculated on the full loan balance. As you pay down principal, the interest portion shrinks and more of your payment goes toward reducing the balance—a key benefit of consistent payments.
