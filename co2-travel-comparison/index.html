Why Compare Travel Emissions?

Global transport systems account for roughly 24% of direct CO2 emissions worldwide. While macro infrastructure moves at its own pace, personal choice offers immediate leverage. By visualizing the true environmental disparity across everyday transportation modes, travelers can shift from passive carbon emitters to active environmental stakeholders.

The Science Behind the Emissions Factors

This platform applies standardized, peer-reviewed emission parameters measured in kilograms of CO2 equivalent per passenger-kilometer (kg CO2e/pkm): Train (0.03), Standard Fuel Car (0.18), and Aviation (0.25). The aviation metric integrates a critical multiplier for Radiative Forcing (RF). RF accounts for non-CO2 atmospheric disruptions such as linear contrails, particulate matter, and nitrous oxides produced at cruise altitude, which multiply the warming velocity compared to ground-level emissions.
