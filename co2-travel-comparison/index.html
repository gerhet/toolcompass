Understanding Travel Emissions

Transportation accounts for nearly 24% of global CO2 emissions, with road vehicles and aviation being the largest contributors. Choosing the right mode of transport for your journey can significantly reduce your personal carbon footprint. Our free CO2 comparator uses scientifically sourced emission factors to help you make informed, eco-conscious travel decisions — instantly and privately.

Emission Factors Used

  • Train: ~0.03 kg CO2 per km (European electric/diesel mix, average occupancy)
  • Car (Petrol): ~0.18 kg CO2 per km (single occupancy, average efficiency)
  • Flight: ~0.25 kg CO2 per km (short-haul average, including radiative forcing)

These values are based on data from the European Environment Agency and ICAO Carbon Calculator. Actual emissions vary by vehicle age, fuel type, occupancy, route efficiency, and flight altitude. For precise carbon accounting in business or research contexts, consult official government environmental databases.

⚠️ Important: This tool provides estimates for personal awareness only. It is not financial, legal, or environmental advice. For official carbon reporting, business compliance, or policy decisions, please use certified tools from environmental agencies or consult qualified sustainability experts.

Trains are generally the most eco-friendly option for land travel, producing significantly less CO2 per kilometer compared to cars and flights. Electric trains powered by renewable energy have the lowest footprint. For longer distances, consider combining train travel with carbon offset programs or choosing direct flights to reduce emissions.

Planes emit CO2 and other greenhouse gases at high altitudes where they have a stronger warming effect (radiative forcing). Short-haul flights are particularly inefficient due to high fuel consumption during takeoff and landing. Additionally, aviation emissions are not always fully accounted for in national carbon budgets, making their true climate impact higher than ground transport.

This calculator provides estimates for personal awareness and planning. For official carbon reporting, business compliance, or academic research, please use certified tools from government environmental agencies or scientific institutions that account for your specific vehicle, route, and operational conditions.
